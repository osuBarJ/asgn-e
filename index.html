Lucille Tenazas is a Filipino-born American graphic designer, educator, and founder of Tenazas Design. She’s played a major role in shaping the arts of graphic design through her mixed medium in typography, language, and photography—to voice her concerns or to persuade her audience the ideas she wishes to convey. Outside of her legacy as a designer, as an educator, she teaches young designers to fundamentally grow their own works of formalism, and imagery with the use of type and technology. This left a mark on the design world as Tenazas’s impact was recognized both in design work and as a leader in design education, which led to a few awards in her design career!
